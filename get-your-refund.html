Why is US Refund contacting me??

Our team of investigators has uncovered information about an ACCOUNT IN YOUR NAME. You should have already been given some detail as to the account type and company that was the source of the account. The account did not have any activity over a period of months or years. The account has been declared “dormant”. The funds are no longer at the institution.

Wait a minute... My money is gone??

Not at all. The money is not gone, but it definitely has been moved.

There’s a law called “escheatment” on the books in all 50 states. It says this: when an institution is holding money that belongs to a customer, and the institution has lost contact with that customer, then the company is not allowed to keep the money and have a party with it…

The institution must close the account, and relinquish the funds to a designated third party for “safekeeping”. So for example, if we wrote to you about your Bank CD — it’s no longer a CD. And the bank no longer has the money. Or if we wrote about a 401(k) you’ve been letting sit… that account has been closed. The money has been moved.

Don’t believe us? We had mentioned which company provided this account information. That’s the institution with whom you had done business, and has surrendered the funds.

Give them a call. We'll wait.

They’re either going to say they have no record of the account, or they’ll explain that the account was closed.

But it’s OK. We can recover these funds for you.

A-Ha! Stealing Personal Information!

(...honestly, we're not...)

So let’s back up a bit.

We told you earlier that your account has been declared “dormant”, and that the funds have been SURRENDERED to a third party for SAFEKEEPING.

So, when you make a claim for your money, you are submitting the claim TO THAT THIRD PARTY.

This third party is actually a government agency... the State Treasurer's Office in the state where your funds are being held.

Now, this state agency can be a paranoid bunch, unfortunately. And their biggest fear is that some fraudster will try to make a claim for money that isn’t really theirs. Because of this, they require that your claim must include certain proof that you are the correct claimant. These can include:

  • ✓ A copy of your driver license. They will then compare the signature on the driver license to the signature on the claim forms.
  • ✓ A copy of a document proving your Social Security Number. Because if the Social Security Number you provide MATCHES the number the agency has on file for this claim, then they feel comfortable you are not a fraudster.

There’s just no way of avoiding it… you’re just going to have to provide your personal information to the state agency that approves your claim. It’s inevitable.

But that doesn’t mean you need to also share your personal information with OUR FIRM.

We don't need it. We don't want it.

That’s why we suggest that you UPLOAD your claim form and any documentation directly to the appproving agency's SECURE WEBSITE (we’ll explain how when the time comes). That’s the safest way to make sure that your personal information is secure. Then just let us know you uploaded it.

(And if you don’t have the equipment to scan and upload documents, we can do it for you, or we can recommend a place such as your local UPS Store which has everything you need)

Bottom Line: If You Want to Keep Your Personal Information Secure, Don’t Share It with Us! (but you may need to share some of your information with the state agency if they ask for it).
